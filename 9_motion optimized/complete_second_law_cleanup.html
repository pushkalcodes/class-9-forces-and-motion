Understanding the Second Law
Newton's Second Law of Motion
The rate of change of momentum of an object is proportional to the applied unbalanced force in the direction of the force. In simpler terms, when a force acts on an object, it changes the object's momentum, and this change depends on both the magnitude of the force and the time during which it acts.
